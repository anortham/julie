## Refreshed breadth corpus with larger verification repos

### WHAT
Replaced the weak `rtk` and `toon-python` breadth/full corpus entries with `riverpod` and `nlohmann-json`, added matching starter matrix cases, and pinned the fixture contract with a new test.

### WHY
The old corpus entries were not earning their keep. They were smaller and not tied to the language verification sweep, which made the breadth matrix less representative than it should be. The harness also had a dead-weight failure mode where swapping repo names alone changed nothing because no matching cases targeted those repos.

### HOW
- Added `search_matrix_contract_tests_starter_fixtures_cover_large_verification_repos_in_breadth` to lock the starter fixtures to `riverpod` and `nlohmann-json`
- Updated `fixtures/search-quality/search-matrix-corpus.toml` breadth/full profiles and repo metadata
- Added repo-targeted cases in `fixtures/search-quality/search-matrix-cases.toml`
- Chose `ProviderContainer` scoped content search for `riverpod` and `json_pointer` definition search for `nlohmann-json`
- Opened the new repos in daemon mode and reran the breadth baseline

### IMPACT
The breadth matrix now exercises 9 live executions with 0 skipped repos, and the replacement repos both produce useful hits instead of sitting in the corpus as inert decoration. This gives better language spread and better baseline signal for future search-quality work.
