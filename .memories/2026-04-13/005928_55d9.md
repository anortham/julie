## Completed Task 5 of workspace-startup-roots plan

Implemented `roots/list_changed` handling and verified that roots refresh/reconciliation is request-bound, policy-aware, and stable.

- **What changed:** `SessionWorkspaceState` now tracks dirty roots refresh state, `on_roots_list_changed` marks roots dirty without rebinding mid-call, the next primary-scoped request refreshes and reconciles roots only for weak `cwd` startup sessions, and explicit CLI/env startup sessions settle back to the startup hint without yielding authority to client roots.
- **Why:** Task 4 gave Julie a first-request roots binding path. Task 5 needed to make later client root changes safe and predictable without surprising explicit-startup sessions or consuming the dirty bit on unrelated secondary-target requests.
- **How:** Added dirty-bit retry behavior, stable empty-roots fallback, explicit-startup authoritative settle behavior, secondary-retention across fallback paths, and primary-vs-secondary wrapper gating for `manage_workspace stats` and other secondary-targeted requests.
- **Impact:** Roots changes are now handled as a deferred state-machine transition at request boundaries, not as an immediate mutation. Weak `cwd` sessions can follow client roots, explicit startup remains authoritative, and secondary workspaces remain usable throughout.
