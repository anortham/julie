## Completed Task 4 of workspace-startup-roots plan

Implemented request-time primary resolution from MCP roots and got it through review.

- **What changed:** Recorded roots capability during `initialize`, deferred `on_initialized` auto-indexing only for weak `Cwd` startup, added request-time `roots/list` primary resolution in `handler.rs`, attached roots-selected primaries through the daemon pool before rebinding, and narrowed wrapper gating to the primary-dependent operations and tool paths that truly need it.
- **Why:** GUI clients with bad `cwd` needed the first real primary-scoped request to bind the correct workspace from client roots instead of trusting the process working directory.
- **How:** Added `ensure_primary_workspace_for_request(...)`, startup-hint fallback on roots failure, daemon attach-before-bind ordering, same-root attach guards, and direct wrapper-gated roots regressions for registry, search, and symbols.
- **Impact:** Weak-`Cwd` daemon sessions can now stay unbound at startup, then reliably bind and use the roots-selected primary on first primary-scoped use without leaking session counts or requiring pre-attached startup workspaces to win by default.
