## Semantic Search Scorecard Dogfood

### WHAT
Added a repeatable semantic-search value scorecard under `docs/eval/semantic-value/` and ran it across 30 cases from 12 local repos/languages: Julie/Rust, Eros/Python, Express/JS, Flask/Python, Gson/Java, Moshi/Kotlin+Java, Alamofire/Swift, Cobra/Go, Sinatra/Ruby, nlohmann-json/C++, Newtonsoft.Json/C#, jq/C.

Also fixed an on-path daemon dogfood failure: cross-repo scorecard runs pushed the daemon into `Too many open files` on macOS because the process inherited a soft `RLIMIT_NOFILE=256`. Added `src/daemon/fd_limit.rs` and daemon startup now raises the soft limit to 4096 when the hard limit permits it.

### WHY
The user is deciding whether Julie's semantic stack earns its maintenance/runtime cost, given Eros is the commercial superset and Julie may remain the OSS fast lexical/graph/token-saving offering. The eval needed real cross-language evidence, not anecdotal single-query dogfood.

### HOW
- Added `README.md`, `scorecard.toml`, and `run_scorecard.py` for lexical/semantic/hybrid `fast_search` comparisons.
- Runner calls `target/release/julie-server tool fast_search` and records top-k/MRR/latency/outcomes.
- Final clean run: `docs/eval/semantic-value/results/2026-05-23T17-47-28Z.{json,md}`.
- Added TDD coverage for daemon FD limit selection in `src/tests/daemon/app_test.rs`.
- Rebuilt release and restarted daemon; log confirmed `Raised daemon file descriptor soft limit old_soft=256 new_soft=4096`.

### IMPACT
Evidence from the final 30-case scorecard favors semantic value on conceptual/code-intent queries:
- Lexical: top5 43.3%, MRR 0.332, p95 101ms
- Semantic: top5 90.0%, MRR 0.759, p95 118ms
- Hybrid: top5 73.3%, MRR 0.614, p95 117ms
- Outcomes: semantic_win 17, lexical_win 1, tie 12

Operational caveat: semantic still has complexity/sidecar cost, but the measured retrieval lift is real on this sample. Also found product-relevant daemon scaling issue and fixed it.
