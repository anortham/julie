## Completed watcher hardening follow-up slice for Track 2

### WHAT
Finished the two watcher follow-up items that were left open after Track 2 Task 4.

- Added a durable `indexing_repairs` SQLite store for persisted repair state.
- Persisted extractor failures from watcher and indexing-pipeline paths instead of only logging them or keeping them in memory.
- Made startup repair planning include persisted extractor-failure state.
- Added watcher replay of persisted extractor-failure repairs.
- Reworked rename handling so destination indexing happens before source retirement, and persisted a durable repair signal when source retirement fails after destination success.

### WHY
The old watcher path still had two dangerous gaps: rename could drop source state before destination indexing proved it was safe, and extractor failures vanished into memory-only repair reasons. That left recovery dependent on luck, logs, or restart timing.

### HOW
- Added `src/database/repairs.rs` plus schema/migration support for `indexing_repairs`.
- Updated watcher handlers to record extractor and rename-retirement repair state durably and clear it on success.
- Updated `startup::plan_primary_workspace_repair()` to read persisted repair reasons.
- Updated `QueueRuntime` to replay persisted extractor repairs and clear them after successful retry.
- Added red-green tests for rename preservation, durable repair persistence, replay through `process_pending_changes()`, startup repair planning, and source-retirement failure persistence.

### IMPACT
- The two explicit watcher follow-up items are no longer unresolved debt.
- Julie can now survive extractor failures and partial rename cleanup failures with durable repair signals instead of losing the trail.
- The next Track 2 indexing hardening step can build on a real persistent repair seam instead of ad hoc watcher state.
