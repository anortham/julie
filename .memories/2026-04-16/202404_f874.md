## Track 4 Task 1 slice: started richer embedding sidecar and service runtime contract

### WHAT
Started the embedding-runtime lane by teaching the sidecar protocol and daemon embedding service to preserve richer runtime detail.

- Added a shared sidecar `HealthResult` contract and Rust validation helper
- Extended Python sidecar test fixtures and protocol tests to assert richer health payload fields
- Threaded health metadata through `SidecarEmbeddingProvider` and exposed provider acceleration and degraded-reason hints
- Changed `EmbeddingServiceSettled` to preserve runtime status in `Ready` and `Unavailable` outcomes instead of flattening to provider-only or reason-only states

### WHY
The old settled service state threw away runtime detail at the point where callers needed it most. That forced indexing and query paths to guess why embeddings were unavailable or degraded, and it made platform-specific behavior, especially CPU fallback and Windows sidecar quirks, harder to reason about.

### HOW
- Added protocol-level `HealthResult` with validation for ready responses
- Updated sidecar runtime metadata to include resolved backend, acceleration, and degraded reason
- Stored sidecar runtime hints on the provider and surfaced them through trait overrides
- Refactored `EmbeddingServiceSettled` into structured variants carrying `EmbeddingRuntimeStatus`
- Updated daemon embedding callers to match the richer settled variants

### IMPACT
Julie now has a stronger runtime contract for embeddings: the sidecar can say more about its state, and the daemon service stops dropping that information on the floor. That gives the next Task 1 slice a solid base for threading capability and degraded-mode truth through init and health surfaces.
