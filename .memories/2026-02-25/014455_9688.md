## Centrality noise filtering — skip boost for ubiquitous trait impls (TDD)

### WHAT
Added `CENTRALITY_NOISE_NAMES` constant and filtering logic to prevent ubiquitous symbols (`to_string`, `clone`, `fmt`, `eq`, `new`, `default`, `from`, `into`, etc.) from receiving centrality score boosts.

### WHY
Symbols like `to_string` (3702 refs) and `clone` (1665 refs) were getting massive centrality boosts that warped search rankings. Their high ref counts reflect language mechanics, not actual importance.

### HOW
- Added `CENTRALITY_NOISE_NAMES` constant (21 names) in `src/search/scoring.rs`
- Added skip check in `apply_centrality_boost()` — affects `fast_search`
- Added skip check in `select_pivots()` in `src/tools/get_context/scoring.rs` — affects `get_context`
- Intentionally separate from `NOISE_NEIGHBOR_NAMES` in pipeline.rs (different purpose, different membership)

### Tests (TDD)
- `test_centrality_boost_skips_noise_names` — `to_string` with ref_score 3702 gets NO boost
- `test_centrality_boost_skips_clone` — `clone` with ref_score 1665 gets NO boost
- `test_select_pivots_centrality_skips_noise_names` — `fmt` not boosted, `format_output` IS boosted
