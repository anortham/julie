## Added English Stemming to CodeTokenizer (TDD)

### WHAT
Added Snowball English stemming to the CodeTokenizer. Morphological variants are now emitted as **additional** tokens alongside exact tokens, enabling cross-variant search matching.

### WHY
Search queries like "token estimation" couldn't find `TokenEstimator` because "estimation" and "estimator" are different strings. With stemming, both produce the stem "estim", enabling the match.

### HOW
- **LazyLock singleton** for the `rust_stemmers::Stemmer` — zero-cost after first access
- **Per-segment stemming**: After all other token variants are emitted (camelCase splits, snake_case splits, affix stripping), stem every token >= 4 chars
- **Deduplication**: Skip stems that equal the original token or are already in the `emitted` HashSet
- **Min length 4**: Avoids noise from stemming short identifiers like "fn", "if", "ok"

### Key Discovery
The Snowball English stemmer does NOT reduce all morphological variants as expected:
- `processor` → `processor` (no change!) but `processing` → `process`
- `runner` → `runner` (no change!) but `running` → `run`
- Fixed test expectations to use pairs that actually share stems: `connected`/`connecting` → `connect`
